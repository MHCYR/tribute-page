About him

Jun Seba (瀬葉淳, Seba Jun, February 7, 1974 – February 26, 2010), born in the Nishi-Azabu district of Minato, was a Japanese record producer, DJ, composer and arranger who recorded under the stage name Nujabes. Nujabes (pronounced /ˈnudʒɑːbɛs/) was an anagram of his name, being the reverse spelling of Seba Jun. His style mixes hip hop and jazz samples.

One of his most recognizable works is that of the background music for Shinichirō Watanabe's series Samurai Champloo (Music Record: Departure and Impression) in 2004.

On February 26, 2010 Jun Seba died in a traffic accident. Following his death, numerous tributes were made by other artists from around the world.
